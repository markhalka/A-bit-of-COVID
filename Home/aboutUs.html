Ghaith

Mohamed Ghaith Majjani started this project as a team building exercise with his cohort and an awareness piece “In the beginning we were watching tv news specials on washing vegetables. Now hardly anyone can be bothered to comply with anything past their legal obligations. This website revisists the rules and regulations we have picked up by showing off Covid in our environment.” Hardly new to the programing, he focused his energy as a project manager; setting up and following up accountability systems, keeping internationals up to date and connecting people to the relative experts of their problems.

Mark

Mark Halka, a CS225 at Waterloo, headed the website development group. His contribution was second only to the documentation every programmer educated themselves from. Our Unity expert, there’s little code that wasn't reviewed, laid out to his advice or produced from his own effort. Equally as important was his experience in leading these types of projects.

Rashmi

Rashmi brought together much of the research for the Interaction with materials simulation. She became knowledgeable in how the virus spreads and is transferred across surfaces. As a developer, Rashimi imported all the models for her group.

Khanh

Khanh helped the Interaction with materials simulation develop equations and ultimately models for demonstrating the survivability of the virus on various surfaces. She collected (research) papers which allowed us to tie in parameters such as temperature and humidity to our calculations, then did her part implementing them as a programer.

Isha

Isha took care of the graphical elements. The varied sprite sheets done by hand and a location to boot; you can thank Isha for the homey appeal of the work.

Ryan

Ryan led the eight bit simulation team, holding everyone together for the journey. He was additionally responsible for the coughing and sneezing functionality, representing the immune system and masks programmatically as well as the main menu that preceded them.

Tom

Tom distinguished himself as the go to person concerning particle physics. Though he started off with the Mask Simulation - working on the UI and fitting the models - he went on to apply himself to all three groups.
